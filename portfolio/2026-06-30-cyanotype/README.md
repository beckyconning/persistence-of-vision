# 2026-06-30 — Cyanotype (the photogram)

Ten pieces in the **cyanotype** medium: UV-sensitised paper turns deep Prussian
blue where light hits it; a specimen laid on top **blocks** the light and prints
as a pale silhouette. No glow-on-black, no additive light — this is a *physical
contact print* with a fixed two-colour chemistry (Prussian blue + paper white)
and soft halos where thin parts of the specimen leak light.

Subjects span what cyanotype historically printed (Anna Atkins' botanicals) and
what the chemistry is uniquely good at (translucent specimens, openwork):

## Self-critique ritual

**1. What was the constraint, and did I hold it?**
A fixed two-tone subtractive chemistry: Prussian-blue exposed ground + paper-white
block, plus a *light-leak halo* where the specimen is thin. Held — every piece is
that palette only, no additive glow, no black. The exposure is deliberately uneven
(blurred value-noise) so it reads as sun on paper, not a flat fill.

**2. What moved along an axis the recent work hadn't?**
Medium again (after halftone-print and watercolor), but the new ground is the
**photogram / contact print**: the image is made by *absence of light*, not paint
or ink. And within it, the standout axis is the **translucent specimen** —
dragonfly wings and lace that print as partial veils, which is the thing cyanotype
does that pigment/halftone cannot fake.

**3. What failed or fought me?**
- The fern first merged into solid white wedges — fixed with much thinner pinnae
  (`pw = plen*0.14`) and a strong forward sweep (`droop=0.95`) so blue shows between leaflets.
- The album-plate "sprig" came out blocky/zipper-like — fixed with alternating
  slim ovate leaves.
- Dragonfly venation is a touch too regular-grid; real venation is irregular polygons.
- Lace fold-leak softening barely reads at this strength.

**4. What's the strongest piece, and why?**
`dragonfly.png`. The translucent wings reading as faint blue veils with darker
cross-veins and pterostigma cells — against the crisp-white opaque body — is the
only piece where the medium's defining property (partial light transmission) *is*
the picture. `lace.png` is a close second for the central rosette + picot edge.

**5. What would the next session do?**
The leak/translucency is the live frontier: a piece built entirely from *degrees*
of translucency (overlapping pressed petals, a folded gauze) so tone comes from
how much light each layer passes — true subtractive depth, no opaque blocks at all.
Also: cyanotype *toning* (tea/tannin shifts blue → violet/brown) for a second hue.
