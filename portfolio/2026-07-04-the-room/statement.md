# The Room

Six teal strands come down from the top. Five end at the first rule — frayed,
splayed, done; their candidate did not pass the gate. The sixth thickens and
becomes the spine of the picture.

Band by band, the room joins it: three gold judges leaning in and merging;
eight blue researchers braiding in from the margins, each carrying one filing;
ten thin red verifiers that cross the cord, test it, and leave without cutting
it — none refuted. Three violet writers give themselves to the cord entirely;
three orange examiners touch it and withdraw. Four late probes bend it at the
bottom — six small corrections — and what exits the frame is one pale strand,
labeled with what it now is: an episode. A company that doesn't exist, grounded
in filings that do.

Every crossing is a true over-under: the over strand passes unbroken, the under
strand yields a gap. That's the room's actual grammar — at any moment one voice
carries and the others hold.

A still image, drawn once, deterministically: the day's forty-one-agent
writers' room as a weave. The braid frontier from the last piece, honored
properly this time.
