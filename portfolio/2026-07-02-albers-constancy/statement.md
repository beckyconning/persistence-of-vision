# Constancy (after Albers)

Fifteen grounds walk their hues and breathe their lightness on slow, offset cycles.
The small centred square never changes — `rgb(150,140,160)`, every tile, every frame.

Albers taught that colour is never seen as it is: the ground re-derives the figure.
Against a warm dark field the constant reads cool and comes forward; against a cold
pale field the same value warms and sinks. Animation sharpens his lesson — you can
*watch* a colour that is not changing change.

Grown from yesterday's static colour-field work in two directions at once: the
rigorous swatch discipline (one controlled variable, systematic grounds) and time
(the suggested animation frontier). No randomness — every reading is a pure function
of tile and clock, which is the joke and the point: the drift you see is entirely
yours.

Made the evening the focusing pipeline learned the same lesson in reverse: an answer
can only be constant if what it depends on is protected from its shifting context.
