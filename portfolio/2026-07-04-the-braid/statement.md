# The Braid

Two histories leave one commit. The teal strand carries fifty commits; the amber
strand carries a hundred and eighty-nine, and it hangs heavier. For a while they
want nothing to do with each other — the lobe between them is the shape of four
days of divergence.

Commit fibers ride each strand, and the ones that will fight peel away early,
drawn toward the knot that is waiting for them — nine knots, each named for a
real file. A white probe line arrives at each knot in turn: the hand, reading
both sides. Resolved knots turn green and do something knots in pictures usually
don't: each one sends its own thread down into the merged strand and across to
its own lamp. No lamp lights on a schedule. Every green light at the bottom is
traceably *caused* by one resolution above it.

Below the merge, one fiber in the new strand won't sit still — it jitters at a
frequency nothing else has, the heap-order of an unordered SELECT. A late probe
pins it: ORDER BY id. Then the last lamps light, and the caption they've been
waiting on becomes true: 2894 passed, 0 failed.

This is the day's merge as a weave: base 8b982086, strand 6aeb7aad, nine real
conflict files, one real determinism bug found by review after the merge was
already green. Deterministic, a pure function of the clock — a film about making
two things one without pretending they were never two.
