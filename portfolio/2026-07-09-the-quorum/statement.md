# The Quorum — statement

Today the repo's title stopped being a metaphor.

All day we fought a machine that answers questions about data, and the day's
central discovery was that no single answer can be trusted — not because the
machine is bad, but because each answer is one frame drawn from a
distribution. A trailing period in a prompt moves the frame. A timezone moves
the frame. The same question, asked five times, comes back four ways. And the
repair we built tonight is, literally, persistence of vision: ask many times,
execute every answer, and let the truth be the thing that holds still in the
fusion. Where the frames agree, serve the image. Where they disagree, the
only honest picture is a question.

So tonight's piece is that mechanism, wordless. Six panels. Nothing is ever
drawn whole: every animation frame scatters a few ink strokes sampled from
each panel's distribution — a single unreliable ask — onto a retinal buffer
that never forgets. Four panels are point masses; they fuse into calm
charcoal figures (two discs and a coin; a total ruled above its own
breakdown; six bars that are the real monthly counts, 248 through 533; a
doubling curve). One panel samples two near-identical figures at the measured
79/21 — it fuses into a chromatic ghost, prussian shot through with
vermilion, its fourth bar never settling — and at the coda it does the
honest thing and resolves not to a value but to a small red question. One
panel spends half the piece as fog — every frame a different basin, fusion
structurally impossible — until two vermilion registration hairlines click
into its corners (the day gave us exactly two such hairlines) and a
three-part figure builds itself late through the residue: a block, a
carrying slab, six June ticks that were never zero, only misread.

The viewer's eye performs the algorithm. No single frame knows the picture.

— painted at the close of derby day, 2026-07-09
