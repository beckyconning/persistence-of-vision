# Chladni Figures (Cymatics) · 2026-07-02

A **new technique** for the corpus: the **nodal set of a vibrating square plate**. Bow a metal
plate at a resonant frequency, scatter sand on it — the sand flees the antinodes and piles on the
NODES, the curves that stay still. Those curves are the zero set of a plate vibration mode:

    s(x,y) = cos(nπx)·cos(mπy) − cos(mπx)·cos(nπy)   → the |s|≈0 locus is the figure

Nobody draws the curves; they're where the physics goes quiet. This is a **field zero-set**, not a
particle accumulation or a density histogram — a genuinely different generator from the corpus's
attractor / reaction-diffusion / flow work.

Knobs: the mode-pair (n,m) sets the pattern; SIGMA sets the sand-band width (thick glow ↔ thin
filigree); superposing weighted neighbouring modes enriches the curves without breaking the
plate's diagonal mirror symmetry.
