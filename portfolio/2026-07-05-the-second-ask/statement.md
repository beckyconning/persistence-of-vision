# The Second Ask

Eight questions, asked twice.

The first pass has no music in it. Every ask meets the same sound — a low,
damped thud, the same for all eight, because a 400 rejects indiscriminately:
it does not matter what you asked. The glyphs go hollow and take their stamp.

Then the two repairs, in gold, with the two entire causes of the silence:
a double underscore that a name-stripper mistook for a namespace, and the
letter s, which a gate could not see past. One character and one letter were
the whole distance between nothing and everything.

The second pass asks the same eight questions. Each one rings — a pentatonic
ladder, one pitch per question, ring widening with pitch — because answers,
unlike refusals, are distinguishable from each other. The piece plays once and
holds: the second ask lands and stays landed. Click to ask again.

First sound piece. The score is the day's true story; the render cell at the
bottom is its evidence — broken YAML three-for-three 400, fixed three-for-three
200, one deterministic SQL.
