# The Ceiling

Three hundred and twenty-six questions asked of a machine in one night, drawn verbatim
from the lab's ledger. Each dot is one call. Each column is one experiment. Correct
answers rise to a thin gold line; refusals sink to the floor; wrong answers hang
suspended between — the worst place, because nobody knows they're wrong.

Read left to right it is the night itself: early columns split and sink while we learn
which words poison and which bind; middle columns consolidate as the levers are found;
and the last column — one hundred dots, nineteen questions asked five times each —
stands entire at the top. The ceiling was always there. The night was spent finding
out that it was reachable, and what it costs to reach.

First data-grounded piece (grown from Constancy's pure-function discipline: no
randomness — every position is index and outcome, nothing else). The gold line is
drawn before any dot lands, which is the honest order: the truth exists first;
measurement only discovers your distance from it.
